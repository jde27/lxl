The matrix exponential

An example: the group U(1)

0.00 Lie theory is based on the observation that we can define an exponential function for matrices in just the same way we can for numbers.

4.50 These three facts will generalise: given a group G of matrices, take the tangent space to G at the identity matrix (and translate it to pass through the zero matrix); the exponentials of these matrices are going to give us elements of G.
